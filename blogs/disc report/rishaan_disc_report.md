The DISC model is a framework for understanding behavioral preferences and communication styles. It is built on four primary factors:

- Steadiness (S): Patient, reliable, calm, and supportive
- Conscientiousness (C): Analytical, detail-oriented, precise, and systematic

Instead, you thrive in environments where expectations are clear, processes are defined, and outcomes can be measured. Your blend of C and D means you are both careful and capable of taking charge when necessary, combining the best of precision and action.

Your natural style—clear, logical, and structured—makes you well-equipped for high-stakes conversations. Below are sample scripts and templates you can adapt for various challenging scenarios:

1. Delivering Constructive Feedback (In-Person or Remote)

> “I’d like to discuss some observations about our recent project. Here’s what I’ve noticed: [state facts]. I think there’s an opportunity to improve by [suggest specific action]. What are your thoughts on this approach?”

5. Navigating a Crisis (Remote/Hybrid Example)
