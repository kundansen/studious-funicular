## **14. Scripts for High-Stakes Conversations**

* **Directing Change:**
  “Here’s the challenge as I see it, and why I think we need to act now. What obstacles do you see, and how can we address them together?”

* **Conflict With a Peer:**
  “I respect your perspective and want to understand it. Here’s where I’m coming from—can we walk through both approaches and see what’s best for the team?”

* **Delegating With Trust:**
  “This is the outcome I’m aiming for. I trust your judgment—let me know if you need anything, but otherwise, you’re empowered to run with it.”

* **Advocating Upwards:**
  “Here’s my rationale for this recommendation, based on data and past results. If there’s a risk I haven’t considered, I want to know now before we move forward.”

* **Giving Constructive Feedback:**
  “I’ve noticed \[specific area] isn’t at our usual standard. Is there anything blocking you? Let’s come up with a plan together to fix this.”

* **Receiving Feedback You Disagree With:**
  “I appreciate you sharing this. I’d like to think about your points and circle back once I’ve reflected.”

* **Navigating Group Deadlock:**
  “We seem to have two strong views here. What if we gather a bit more data or pilot both approaches—then regroup with results?”

High-stakes conversations are a hallmark of effective leadership—and your DISC profile gives you a unique edge in navigating them. Because you’re naturally direct, logical, and action-oriented, you tend to approach these moments with clarity and a solutions mindset. However, you also understand that the right words, tone, and timing can turn a challenging exchange into a moment of trust, progress, and even inspiration.

**When directing change,** you instinctively tie the conversation to the “why” behind the initiative. You’re transparent about challenges and quick to invite input—making people feel invested rather than dictated to. Your style signals: “This is important, and I trust us to figure it out together.”

**In moments of disagreement or pushback,** you avoid personal attacks and instead focus on the issue, not the individual. Your use of logic, data, and respectful challenge helps keep tempers cool and minds open. You’re also not afraid to call a timeout—recognizing when emotions are running high and suggesting a break to regroup.

**When delegating,** you balance empowerment with accountability. Your words reinforce trust, but also set a clear standard for follow-through. If someone is struggling or missing the mark, your feedback is firm but never belittling; you offer support and partnership in finding a solution.

**In advocating upwards or with leadership,** you lead with evidence and impact. You anticipate objections and invite constructive criticism, showing you value the big picture—not just your own viewpoint.

**Across all scenarios,** your style is to drive towards action while preserving (or even strengthening) relationships. You understand that the goal of any high-stakes conversation is not just resolution, but building alignment and momentum for what comes next.

**Consider tailoring your approach for each audience:**

* With a dominant or analytical audience, emphasize results, facts, and plans.
* With relational or steady types, include reassurance, context, and patience.
* In all cases, express curiosity and openness: “What am I missing?” or “How do you see it?”

By preparing for these moments—not just with facts, but with empathy and adaptability—you maximize your influence and ensure your message lands as intended.
