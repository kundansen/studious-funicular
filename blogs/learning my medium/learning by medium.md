## Section 1: Media Face-Off—Books vs. Audio vs. Video (and the Speed Reading Debate)
Let’s crunch the numbers on how fast we learn and retain across media, with a detour into speed reading’s claims.

- **Books: The Slow Burn.** Reading clocks in at 200-300 words per minute (wpm) on average, per a 2025 Tech Learning Institute study, dropping to 150 wpm for dense topics like architecture. Comprehension peaks at 70-80% with focused reading, but speed reading—pushed by Edward Tufte as outpacing listening—claims 400-700 wpm. A 2016 Psychological Science study debunks this: retention plummets to 50% beyond 300-500 wpm as subvocalization (that inner voice) falters. Memory thrives with active note-taking—adding 10-15% recall, per a 2023 Journal of Educational Psychology paper. A dev quipped, “I retained more doodling than speed-reading page 87 of a refactoring book.”

- **Audiobooks: The Speed Demon.** At normal speed, audiobooks match reading at 200-300 wpm, but 1.5x hits 300-450 wpm, and 2x reaches 400-600 wpm (2025 Audible data). Comprehension holds at 60-70% at 1.5x, dropping to 40-50% at 2x due to auditory overload. Retention boosts 15-20% with repetition, per a 2024 Cognitive Science review. Memory sticks best with emotional narration—think a passionate GenAI explainer—adding 10% recall. Perfect for “Soft Skills for Engineers” on commutes, if you re-listen.

- **Podcasts: The Quick Hit.** At 1x, podcasts run 120-150 wpm, but 1.5x-2x pushes to 180-300 wpm. Comprehension hovers at 55-65% at 1.5x, per 2025 Spotify analytics, dipping to 40% at 2x. Retention jumps 12% with interactive pauses (e.g., reflecting on “The Changelog”). Memory forms best with storytelling—narrative podcasts on software delivery outscore lectures by 8%, per a 2022 Memory & Cognition study. A PM joked, “2x chipmunk mode taught me GenAI buzz, barely.”

- **Training Videos: The Visual Edge.** Videos average 120-180 wpm at 1x, scaling to 180-360 wpm at 1.5x-2x (2025 Coursera stats). Comprehension hits 65-75%, 25% higher than books for refactoring, thanks to visuals. Retention reaches 70% with rewatching key segments, per a 2023 eLearning Journal. Memory spikes 15-20% with hands-on practice post-viewing—e.g., coding along with a demo.

- **Video Podcasts: The Hybrid Beast.** At 1.5x-2x, these hit 180-300 wpm, with 60-70% comprehension (2025 YouTube data). Retention edges to 65% with visual cues, per a 2024 Visual Learning Review. Memory improves 10% with dual coding—seeing and hearing “Coding with Chris” on Kubernetes. One viewer noted, “Learned fast, memorized their coffee orders too.”

Each medium’s a tool—books for depth with notes, audio for speed with repetition, video for clarity with practice. Speed reading’s a myth for deep learning; balance trumps haste.
