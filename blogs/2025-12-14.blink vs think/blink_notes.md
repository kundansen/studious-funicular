## 20 Notable Quotes from *Blink*

### On Thin-Slicing & Rapid Cognition
1.  "Thin-slicing refers to the ability of our unconscious to find patterns in situations and behavior based on very narrow slices of experience."
2.  "There can be as much value in the blink of an eye as in months of rational analysis."
3.  "Decisions made very quickly can be every bit as good as decisions made cautiously and deliberately."
4.  "We live in a world that assumes that the quality of a decision is directly related to the time and effort that went into making it."
5.  "In the first two seconds of looking—in a single glance—they were able to understand more about the essence of the statue than the team at the Getty was able to understand after fourteen months."
6.  "Anyone who has ever scanned the bookshelves of a new girlfriend or boyfriend... understands this implicitly; you can learn as much – or more – from one glance at a private space as you can from hours of exposure to a public face."
7.  "A person watching silent two-second video clips of a teacher... will reach conclusions about how good that teacher is that are very similar to those of a student who has sat in the teacher's class for an entire semester."

### On The Adaptive Unconscious
8.  "The adaptive unconscious does an excellent job of sizing up the world, warning people of danger, setting goals, and initiating action in a sophisticated and efficient manner."
9.  "Our power of thin-slicing and snap judgment are extraordinary. But even the giant computer in our unconscious needs a moment to do its work."
10. "We need to respect the fact that it is possible to know without knowing why we know and accept that - sometimes - we're better off that way."
11. "Our unconscious is a powerful force. But it's fallible. It's not the case that our internal computer always shines through, instantly decoding the 'truth' of a situation."
12. "The only way that human beings could ever have survived as a species for as long as we have is that we've developed another kind of decision-making apparatus that's capable of making very quick judgments based on very little information."

### On The Limits of Introspection (The Storytelling Problem)
13. "We have, as human beings, a storytelling problem. We're a bit too quick to come up with explanations for things we don't really have an explanation for."
14. "When we ask people to explain their thinking, we are asking them to do something that they are not really capable of doing."
15. "Insight is not a lightbulb that goes off inside our heads. It is a flickering candle that can easily be snuffed out."

### On Bias & The Warren Harding Error
16. "The disturbing thing about the test is that it shows that our unconscious attitudes may be utterly incompatible with our stated conscious values."
17. "The Warren Harding error is the dark side of rapid cognition. It is at the root of a good deal of prejudice and discrimination."

### On Expertise & Training
18. "Truly successful decision making relies on a balance between deliberate and instinctive thinking."
19. "Expertise means that your adaptive unconscious is capable of making very quick judgments based on very little information."
20. "We can teach ourselves to think without thinking."
