## Suggested publication blurb (150–200 words)
Malcolm Gladwell calls it the "rule of 150." Robin Dunbar discovered it through primate neuroscience. Large organizations keep ignoring it. This article traces how Dunbar's insight—that group size is limited by cognitive capacity for maintaining relationships—became Gladwell's organizational principle: teams larger than ~150 people lose trust, communication, and context. The solution isn't more process; it's deliberate structure. Small, autonomous teams. Local norms. A few well‑placed connectors. W.L. Gore built a $10B company on this principle. So did the Roman Army. Most modern companies still treat size and scale as separate problems. Read this if you lead teams, build organizations, or wonder why large groups feel broken even when the people are good.
