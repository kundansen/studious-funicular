### Color Palette & Ink Mixing

**Primary Palette**:
- **Sumi ink blacks**: 4-5 dilution levels from pure black (outlines, plate rim) to barely-there gray (distant steam)
- **Warm grays**: Mix ink with slight sepia/warm brown undertone for shadows on curry and luchi
- **Golden ochre**: For luchi highlights—mix yellow ochre with burnt sienna, apply sparingly where oil gleams
- **Pale cream**: For shada torkari—very diluted warm white with hint of yellow, applied in controlled washes
- **Bengal gram yellow**: Tiny dots of pure yellow ochre for dal pieces
- **Ambient warmth**: Faint peachy-cream wash in lower right (suggesting warm oil, morning light on brass)

**Color Application Zones**:
- 70% traditional sumi-e (blacks, grays, negative space)
- 20% selective warm accents (luchi golden tones, curry cream)
- 10% atmospheric color (background window light, environmental warmth)

### Brush Techniques & Execution Notes

**For Brass Plate & Utensils**:
- **Outline**: Confident, varied-pressure stroke (thicker at bottom for weight, thinner at rim)
- **Texture**: Dry-brush horizontal strokes suggesting circular hammering marks on brass
- **Highlights**: Leave white paper showing through OR use very pale ink for reflective spots
- **Patina**: Small irregular darker patches (aged brass character)

**For Luchi (Fried Bread)**:
- **Form**: Start with light circular wash, add darker edges
- **Blistered texture**: Fine dry-brush stippling (load brush partially, drag across paper to create broken texture)
- **Puffed dome**: Lighter wash at center (highest point), gradual darkening toward edges
- **Fried crispy edges**: Darker ochre-brown around circumference
- **Oil sheen**: Minimal golden highlights (restrained—avoid making it look greasy)
- **Torn luchi**: Show internal hollow with darker shadow inside, irregular tear marks

**For Shada Torkari (White Curry)**:
- **Base**: Pale cream wash, slightly uneven (hand-painted, not perfect)
- **Potatoes**: Soft-edged beige-cream shapes, slightly lighter than curry liquid
- **Bengal gram**: Tiny distinct dots in yellow-ochre (3-5 visible pieces)
- **Liquid consistency**: Gradient from opaque (thicker areas) to semi-transparent (pooling edges)
- **Surface**: Very subtle highlights suggesting liquid sheen (mustard oil)
- **Depth**: Darker wash at bowl interior edges to show three-dimensionality

**For Steam**:
- **Technique**: Vertical brushstrokes with lifting motion (pressing down, pulling up, lifting off)
- **Ink dilution**: Start medium-dark at base, progressively dilute as you stroke upward
- **Stroke variety**: Mix thin calligraphic lines with broader wash-like wisps
- **Direction**: Generally upward but with organic curves and spirals
- **Rhythm**: 2-3 dominant steam trails with several smaller supporting wisps
- **Integration**: Steam should connect curry/kadai to upper atmosphere, bridging composition

**For Background Figures**:
- **Silhouette wash**: Single application, medium-dilution ink, no reworking
- **Edges**: Soft, slightly blurred (suggesting motion or depth of field)
- **Detail level**: Minimal—shoulders, head shape, general posture only
- **Integration**: Should recede visually, not compete with foreground food

### Cultural Authenticity Details

**Brass Utensils**:
- Traditional kangsho-bati has slight rim curl, hammered texture
- Katori (small bowl) often has pedestal base or thick rim
- Patina and wear marks suggest family heirlooms, not new items

**Luchi Shape & Texture**:
- Not perfectly round (hand-rolled dough)
- Characteristic balloon-puff when fried properly
- Blistered surface from oil bubbles
- Edges slightly darker (fried longer)
- Tears reveal hollow interior (key identifying feature)

**Shada Torkari Appearance**:
- "White" is relative—actually pale cream to light beige
- No turmeric means no yellow/orange tones
- Potatoes: soft-cooked, slightly broken edges
- Bengal gram: maintains distinct shape, doesn't fully dissolve
- Consistency: Medium-thick, coats spoon but isn't soup-like

**Morning Ritual Context**:
- Sunday breakfast means leisurely timing (no rush implied in composition)
- Multiple luchis suggest family meal, not single serving
- Presence of background figures suggests communal preparation/eating
- Steam indicates just-cooked, served hot (temporal specificity)

### Technical Execution Sequence

1. **Rough sketch**: Light pencil marks for plate position, luchi arrangement, steam flow paths
2. **Background washes**: Apply pale window light, atmospheric background first (lightest values)
3. **Background figures**: Single-pass silhouettes while previous wash still slightly damp (soft edges)
4. **Brass plate outline**: Confident single stroke, let dry
5. **Curry bowl & contents**: Base wash, then details (potatoes, dal) while damp
6. **Luchi forms**: Light base wash, let dry, add texture with dry-brush
7. **Luchi accents**: Golden ochre highlights, darker fried edges
8. **Steam**: Vertical calligraphic strokes, varying dilutions, work quickly while spontaneous
9. **Plate details**: Texture, patina, highlights last
10. **Final touches**: Darkest darks (plate rim, curry bowl interior), brightest highlights (oil sheen)

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't**:
- Make it too literal/photographic (loses sumi-e poetry)
- Overwork the washes (muddy, loses freshness)
- Add too much color (should be 70% traditional ink)
- Make steam mechanical or symmetrical
- Include modern elements (plastic, stainless steel, printed patterns)
- Center the composition (loses dynamic asymmetry)
- Make background figures too detailed (they should recede)
- Use harsh outlines everywhere (vary line weight and softness)

✅ **Do**:
- Embrace negative space (as important as painted areas)
- Vary ink dilutions (create depth through value range)
- Let brushstrokes show (celebrate technique, not hide it)
- Keep color accents strategic and restrained
- Suggest detail rather than render everything
- Maintain soft, organic edges (except key sharp details)
- Let paper white work as highlight
- Create atmospheric depth through value and edge control
