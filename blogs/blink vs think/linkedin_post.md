# LinkedIn Post Options for "Blink vs Think"

Here are three options for your LinkedIn post, each with a different "hook" strategy.

## Option 1: The "Counter-Intuitive" Hook
*Best for: Sparking debate and challenging the "data-driven" status quo.*

**Hook:**
Production doesn't wait for your dashboard to refresh. Sometimes, the most "data-driven" thing you can do is ignore the data and trust your gut.

**Body:**
We're taught that good engineering decisions require endless metrics, A/B tests, and analysis. But in a crisis, or when reviewing complex code, your brain's pattern recognition (what Malcolm Gladwell calls "thin-slicing") is often faster and more accurate than any dashboard.

The trick isn't to guess—it's to know *when* you've earned the right to blink, and when you absolutely need to think.

## Option 2: The "Expertise" Hook
*Best for: Resonating with senior engineers and leaders.*

**Hook:**
A senior engineer can spot a broken design in 20 seconds. A junior engineer needs 2 hours to find the same flaw. That's not magic—it's compression.

**Body:**
We call it "intuition," but it's actually a mental cache built from hundreds of pull requests and late-night incidents. It's the ability to "thin-slice" a problem and see the outcome before the code even runs.

But there's a danger: that same fast-thinking engine is where bias lives. If you trust your gut blindly, you're just automating your prejudices.

## Option 3: The "Framework" Hook
*Best for: Providing immediate value and actionable advice.*

**Hook:**
Stop treating every decision like a math problem. Some choices deserve a spreadsheet; others just need a pulse check. Do you know which is which?

**Body:**
I use a simple 2x2 framework to decide whether to "Blink" (decide fast) or "Think" (analyze deep):

1.  **Stakes:** High or Low?
2.  **Reversibility:** Can we undo this?
3.  **Expertise:** Have I seen this 100 times before?

If it's reversible and you're an expert: **Blink**.
If it's irreversible and new territory: **Think**.

Getting this wrong is why we have analysis paralysis on button colors and reckless "gut calls" on system architecture.
