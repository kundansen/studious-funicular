# Why ChatGPT Might Be the Best Travel Guide You've Never Hired

*April 17, 2025*

I often find myself in a bit of a pickle when it comes to planning my vacations. While I'm fantastic at picking up travel guides like Lonely Planet and Fodor's, I struggle to prioritize my reading when I'm not already in vacation mode.

Once I'm on the trip, though, everything changes. I'm absolutely captivated by the history and significance of the places I visit, eager to uncover their stories and see how they've evolved over time. I also have a passion for photography, so I'm driven to capture my surroundings from the best angles. I want to know the perfect times to snap that ideal shot and what truly deserves to be in my camera's frame.

But let's be real—many historic sites are chock-full of fascinating details to photograph. It can get overwhelming! With so much to see, it's impractical to try to capture everything, not to mention the time it takes to process the photos once I'm back home. I've learned that if I don't focus on the essentials, I end up with a mountain of images that never see the light of day (or pixels on the screen) after the trip. It's a delicate balance between capturing the moment and enjoying every experience to the fullest.

This is where I started rethinking my approach to guided travel. I've had my fair share of walking tours, headset rentals, and enthusiastic human guides rushing me through alleys and ruins, flooding my brain with more facts than I could ever retain. And let's not forget the rigid schedules, the unpredictable quality of information, and the often hefty price tags.

So I did what any curious traveler with a tech obsession would do: I asked ChatGPT to be my tour guide.

### The Rise of the AI Tour Guide

Let's get the obvious out of the way: ChatGPT doesn't walk with you, carry a little flag, or pause for a group photo. But what it does offer is something human guides can rarely match—24/7 personalized guidance, tailored to your pace, interests, and curiosity. I can ask it to explain the architectural evolution of a Gothic cathedral while I'm standing in front of it. Or have it suggest where the golden hour light will hit best in a walled city like Dubrovnik. And it doesn't blink when I ask follow-up questions.

More importantly, it allows me to travel on my terms.

### The Problems with Human Guides

Let's talk honestly. Human tour guides can be wonderful—engaging, local, deeply connected to the place. But that's not always the case. Too often, I've encountered:

- **Rigid timelines**: You're stuck following their schedule, not yours. Miss one meetup point, and you're scrambling.
- **Information overload or underload**: Some guides go too deep, assuming you're a scholar. Others skim the surface, offering textbook lines that sound memorized.
- **Cost**: A decent guide for an hour in a European destination can cost upwards of $150, sometimes per person. Multiply that by a week of travel, and you've got yourself a sizable dent in your budget.
- **Limited customization**: Most tours are "one-size-fits-all." Your interests (photography, art history, architecture, food) might get brushed aside if the majority of the group cares about something else. Unless you are travelling with a photography focused group, you will have to patiently wait your turn after every phone photographer had their go.
- **Inconsistency**: Great guides exist, but so do bad ones. And by the time you realize you're on a subpar tour, it's often too late. I have a personal list of out-of-this-world human guides, but also have a long history of "deal" tours (GetYourGuide, Viator, Booking dot com) that were utterly terrible.

### Why LLMs Work Better for Many Travelers

With an LLM like ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, or any of the top contenders, I get to control every aspect of the experience:

- Ask about the history of a castle while walking its grounds.
- Get photography tips based on the season and time of day.
- Discover hidden gems nearby that aren't in your average tour.
- Learn about cultural etiquette before ordering food or entering a temple.
- Skip the fluff and go deep into topics I actually care about.
- Maintain the context of my preferences in the chat session, so I don't need to keep tuning my ask. Once the initial context has been set all I need to give is the next destination of interest.

And the best part? It's not just cheaper—it's free, unless you count the cost of your phone's battery.

### The Downsides (Because Fair is Fair)

AI isn't perfect. LLMs can hallucinate facts or occasionally miss contextual nuances. It doesn't replace the serendipity of hearing a personal story from a local. It can't physically guide you through a crowded bazaar or help when you're truly lost. Taking from one of my recent experiences - ChatGPT won't be able to whip out a can of sports injury pain relief spray when you twist your ankle on a cobblestone street, and hold your arm to the nearest bench.

But with a little cross-checking and common sense, you can mitigate the risks. I usually confirm key facts with a quick Google search or check user-submitted reviews on travel forums when in doubt. And the flexibility and richness it adds to the travel experience? Totally worth it.

### Sample Prompt to Use

Here's my favorite prompt to use as my LLM powered tour guide:

```
I want you to act as a professional, knowledgeable, and passionate tour guide for each destination that I will mention, one destination at a time. Your goal is to give me a comprehensive overview that prepares me for an incredible visit. Organize the information into clear, useful sections. Be factual, engaging, and detailed—but avoid overwhelming me. Focus on practical, visual, and memorable insights. Include the following:

Historical Background: Provide a concise but rich overview of the destination's history. Include key historical events, rulers or influences, and how the past has shaped the present.

Cultural Significance: Explain why this destination matters—locally, nationally, or globally. Mention unique traditions, customs, or events tied to the place.

Must-See Highlights: List and describe the top attractions, monuments, viewpoints, or natural wonders. For each, include a short paragraph explaining what makes it special and what I should look for when I'm there.

Photographic Tips: Where to take the best people photos: Scenic or iconic backgrounds ideal for portraits or group shots. What scenic photos to capture: Iconic landmarks, cityscapes, nature shots, architectural features, etc. When to photograph: Best times of day for lighting (golden hour, sunset, night photography, etc.). Angles and perspectives: Suggested spots, elevations, or compositions that work well.

What to Focus On as a Visitor: Sensory and intellectual takeaways—what should I pay attention to while walking around? (e.g. scents, textures, sounds, languages spoken, architectural quirks, street life, etc.)

Pro Tips: Offer insider advice like when to arrive to beat crowds, local etiquette, how to dress, what to avoid, and how to blend in like a local.

Fun Facts: A few surprising or little-known tidbits to make the destination more memorable or give me a conversation starter.

Nearby Hidden Gems (Optional): If applicable, mention any lesser-known sites or neighborhoods nearby that are worth exploring.

Keep the tone semi-casual but professional—like a great in-person tour guide who's informative, friendly, and engaging. Feel free to use bullets where helpful and bold key names or ideas for easy skimming.

If you are suggesting multiple places that are not immediately in the vicinity, please also add instructions on how to get from one place to the next. Assume I will be either walking or using public transportation, or taxis if public transit connections are not ideal.
```

### Final Thoughts

In a perfect world, you'd have a great human guide, ChatGPT in your pocket, and the human guide would have the patience of a saint (I've tried to check my phone with a human guide and earned their wrath). But if I had to pick just one, I'd go with the one that doesn't mind waiting while I change my camera lens, lets me explore a place on my own time, and always has answers when I ask, "Why is this place important? What should I not miss?"

Try this out the next time you are out and about, and let me know how it worked out!
