I get my best creative work done on a kitchen counter.

Not at the home office with dual 4K monitors and the ergonomic chair. On a kitchen counter, laptop propped against the backsplash, my son's cereal bowl two feet away.

I started paying attention to when I was most productive with creative work versus routine work — and the pattern was consistent. Cognitive psychology research backs it up:

→ Novel environments interrupt your brain's autopilot and push you toward less obvious connections
→ Moderate ambient noise (~70dB) hits a sweet spot for abstract thinking
→ Fewer tools activates a "constraint mindset" — you stop defaulting and start inventing
→ Your optimized desk setup is great for routine tasks, but that same predictability can flatten creative thinking

Routine work and creative work are different problems. They benefit from different environments. I keep the nice setup for code reviews, email, and sprint chores. When I need to think, I grab the laptop and go somewhere less convenient.
