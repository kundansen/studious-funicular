Two things collided in my reading this week: Anthropic's new labor market research and Malcolm Gladwell's Revenge of the Tipping Point.

The pattern that emerged is Gladwell's "Law of the Very, Very, Very Few" — playing out in real-time across the tech workforce.

A small number of builders are achieving extraordinary productivity gains with AI. A small number of reckless practitioners are flooding codebases with slop. A large middle is drifting through administrative work that AI will happily consume.

The data is striking:
→ Top AI adopters: +61% deployed code. Bottom adopters (same tools, same org): -11%
→ 94% theoretical AI exposure in tech. Actual impact? A fraction of that.
→ Entry-level dev positions: down 60% since 2022
→ 45% of AI-generated code fails security tests

The gap between Gladwell's productive Few and the destructive Very Few? Discipline, not willingness. Both adopted early, but the gap between them comes down to discipline — one group builds with architectural rigor while the other vibe-codes without guardrails.

The path forward is specific: pick up a prototype. Rubber-duck a design decision with AI. Convert that decade-old tech debt into a modern stack. The adjacent possible just got a lot more adjacent.
