# Positive Aspects of Teaching Online During COVID-19: Zoom Backgrounds, MannyCam, and Increased Student Engagement
**Author:** Daniel A. Russell  
**Publication Date:** August 09, 2021


## Introduction
- **Context:** The COVID‑19 pandemic forced courses to move online, requiring instructors to quickly adapt their teaching methods.
- **Focus:** The paper reflects on positive experiences from teaching online via Zoom—highlighting tools, strategies, and unexpected benefits that the author hopes to retain when returning to in-person teaching.

## 1. When COVID‑19 Moved My Teaching Online
- **Timeline:** Teaching shifted entirely online from March 2020 through May 2021.
- **Transition:** The rapid move required faculty to teach from home, with courses delivered over Zoom.
- **Challenge & Opportunity:** While the change brought significant challenges, it also opened up new ways of interacting with students.

## 2. Teaching with Zoom from a Classroom
- **Multimedia Classroom Setup:** Prior to COVID‑19, lectures were broadcast from a well-equipped classroom with multiple cameras, monitors, and a dedicated student producer.
- **Contrast:** This professional setup ensured high-quality production and reliable student interaction.

## 3. Teaching with Zoom from My Work‑From‑Home Office
- **Home Studio Setup:** With campus closures, the author transitioned to teaching from a dedicated home office.
  - **Initial Setup:** Started with a basic dual-laptop system.
  - **Upgrades:** Over time, the setup improved—adding a high-quality microphone, ring light, larger external monitor, and eventually a standing desk.
- **Outcome:** These improvements allowed the instructor to closely mimic a classroom experience, enhancing visual and audio quality.

## 4. Teaching to a Camera or to Student Faces?
- **Interaction Challenge:** Teaching online initially felt impersonal because of the lack of immediate, physical feedback from students.
- **Engagement Shift:** As more students turned on their cameras, the instructor noticed a significant boost in engagement and the ability to interact more naturally.
- **Feedback Value:** Seeing student faces provided critical real-time feedback, improving the teaching experience.

## 5. Minimal Changes to My Teaching Style
- **Continuity:** Despite the move online, the author was able to maintain his teaching style.
- **Consistency:** The delivery, use of partially complete notes, and even the method of annotating lecture material remained largely similar to in-person teaching.
- **Student Reception:** Many students appreciated the continuity and consistency of instruction during the transition.

## 6. Bringing Humor into a Difficult Situation
### A. Green Screens and Zoom Virtual Backgrounds
- **Innovation:** Shortly after moving online, the author discovered Zoom’s virtual backgrounds.
- **Creative Approach:** He used a green screen combined with fun costumes and themed backgrounds (e.g., Princess Leia, Wayne Campbell, Indiana Jones) to inject humor and creativity into lectures.
- **Impact:** Students enjoyed the playful, humorous approach, with many capturing screenshots of the daily surprises.

### B. The Adventures of Manfred Stiffbody (Manny the Mannequin)
- **Manny the Mannequin:** To add a unique, recurring humorous element, the author repurposed an art mannequin from his lab.
- **MannyCam:** A second webcam (the "MannyCam") was used to show close-up views of Manny’s daily poses during lectures.
- **Student Reaction:** Manny’s antics became an engaging “easter egg” that students looked forward to, enhancing the overall classroom experience.

### C. Daily Polls as Icebreakers
- **Interactive Polls:** Daily Zoom poll questions were used to spark conversation and encourage student interaction.
- **Topics:** Fun questions (e.g., preferences between Macs or PCs, favorite movies or coffee flavors) helped break the ice.
- **Engagement:** Polls were well received by both resident and distance education students, contributing to a more dynamic and inclusive online environment.

## 7. Classroom Demonstrations Didn’t Work Over Zoom
- **Challenge:** The author found that elaborate physical demonstrations, which are effective in person, did not translate well online.
- **Workaround:** Instead, pre-recorded videos and clips were used, though these did not fully replace the interactive quality of live demonstrations.
- **Future Direction:** The author plans to produce high-quality video demonstrations that could be used in future online or hybrid settings.

## 8. Reflections on Teaching Over Zoom
### A. Equity in Student Interactions
- **In-Person vs. Online:** In traditional classrooms, resident students typically dominate interactions. Online, however, all students (resident and distance) had equal opportunity to engage.
- **Enhanced Interaction:** The Zoom format allowed the instructor to see and interact with all students, which greatly increased overall engagement.
- **Visual Grid Benefit:** The ability to view a grid of student faces improved the instructor’s ability to address questions and foster discussion.

### B. Increased Interaction Between Resident and Distant Students
- **Online Study Groups:** The online environment fostered new collaborations between resident and distance education students.
- **Discussion Boards:** Increased activity on online discussion platforms also contributed to higher levels of interaction and learning.
- **Future Plans:** The instructor intends to maintain some form of online collaborative space (e.g., Gather.Town) even after returning to in-person classes.

### C. One-on-One Chats with Students
- **Personal Interaction:** Scheduling private Zoom meetings allowed for deeper, individual connections with students.
- **Positive Feedback:** A large number of students signed up for these one-on-one chats, providing the instructor with valuable insights and personal rapport.
- **Ongoing Practice:** The instructor plans to continue offering personal interaction opportunities in both online and in-person formats.

## 9. Conclusion: Things I Hope to Retain When I Return to an In-Person Classroom
- **Hybrid Model:** The instructor intends to combine the best aspects of online and in-person teaching.
- **Humor and Engagement:** While some elements (like virtual backgrounds and MannyCam) may not directly transfer, the creative, humorous approaches and daily interactive practices (like polls) will be retained.
- **Enhanced Interaction:** The equitable engagement achieved online will inform future classroom strategies—ensuring both resident and distance students can interact meaningfully.
- **Future Improvements:** Plans include creating high-quality demonstration videos, integrating online discussion tools into the classroom, and continuing personalized engagement with students.

## Overall Reflections
- **Unexpected Benefits:** Despite challenges, teaching online revealed new methods to boost student engagement and interaction.
- **Personal Growth:** The experience was not only educational for students but also therapeutic and creatively fulfilling for the instructor.
- **Long-Term Impact:** The lessons learned during the pandemic are expected to improve teaching practices in future hybrid or in-person settings.



## Key Information on Zoom Backgrounds and Their Effects on Audience Perception

### Overview
- **Objective:**  
  The paper reflects on the positive aspects of teaching online during the COVID-19 pandemic, with a particular focus on how creative use of Zoom backgrounds (and related tools like MannyCam) can enhance student engagement.
- **Context:**  
  Amid the abrupt shift from in-person to online teaching, the author describes his experiences using virtual backgrounds and humorous visual elements to overcome the challenges of remote instruction.
- **Method:**  
  This is a reflective, experiential study that draws on the author’s personal teaching experiences during the pandemic, supported by classroom observations and anecdotal evidence.

### Zoom Backgrounds and Their Impact on Perception

#### Enhancing Engagement Through Creative Backgrounds
- **Use of Virtual Backgrounds:**  
  The author experimented with a wide variety of themed virtual backgrounds (e.g., Princess Leia aboard the Tantive IV, Wayne Campbell, Indiana Jones) combined with costumes. This creative approach was used to infuse humor and personality into his lectures.
- **Effect on Student Engagement:**  
  These backgrounds helped make the online classes more entertaining and engaging. Students enjoyed the novelty and even took screenshots to share with others.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "Coming up with new ideas for costumes and backgrounds was tremendously therapeutic for me during an otherwise stressful period, and my students loved it — several even took daily screenshots to share with their friends."

#### Balancing Professionalism and Authenticity
- **Trade-offs in Background Selection:**  
  While playful virtual backgrounds increased engagement, there was an awareness of the need to balance this creativity with maintaining a professional appearance. Not every context may call for highly personalized or humorous backgrounds.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "I ended up having a lot of fun with Zoom backgrounds and costumes; however, I recognize that such playful elements may not be suitable for all teaching contexts and must be balanced against maintaining a professional image."

#### The Role of MannyCam and Personalized Visual Cues
- **MannyCam:**  
  In addition to virtual backgrounds, the instructor introduced “MannyCam” – a secondary webcam showing a small art mannequin (nicknamed Manny) placed in the background. Manny’s changing poses added an element of surprise and humor.
- **Impact on Perceptions:**  
  The presence of MannyCam provided a unique visual cue that enhanced the sense of connection and interactivity in the virtual classroom.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "Students told me they would take screenshots of Manny’s antics; it became an endearing symbol of our unique online learning experience."

### Broader Implications for Online Teaching
- **Enhanced Interaction:**  
  The creative visual strategies not only increased student engagement but also fostered a greater sense of community among both resident and distance learners.
- **Retention of Effective Online Practices:**  
  Although some elements (e.g., Zoom backgrounds and MannyCam) may be challenging to replicate in traditional classrooms, the author expresses a desire to integrate elements of these engaging practices into future teaching.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "While I won’t be able to replicate the Zoom backgrounds and costumes in a physical classroom, the increased engagement and positive feedback from students are aspects I hope to carry forward."

*Summary:*  
Daniel A. Russell’s paper demonstrates that the creative use of Zoom backgrounds and innovative tools like MannyCam can positively influence student engagement and shape favorable perceptions in an online learning environment. The study highlights how such visual strategies can inject humor, foster connection, and create a dynamic virtual classroom—while also cautioning that the balance between creativity and professionalism is key.
