# Virtual First Impressions: Zoom Backgrounds Affect Judgements of Trust and Competence
**Authors:** Abi Cook, Meg Thompson, Paddy Ross  
**Publication Date:** September 27, 2023

## Abstract
- **Overview:** The study investigates how non-verbal cues from video backgrounds—along with facial expressions and gender—influence first impressions of trustworthiness and competence in videoconferencing settings.
- **Key Findings:** 
  - Background type significantly affects perceptions.
  - Backgrounds featuring plants and bookcases yield higher ratings for both trust and competence.
  - Home and novelty backgrounds are rated lower.
  - Happy facial expressions boost both trustworthiness and competence ratings.
  - Female faces are generally judged as more trustworthy and, in many cases, more competent than male faces.

## Introduction
- **First Impressions:** First impressions are formed rapidly, and these judgments play a critical role in social interactions and decision-making.
- **Videoconferencing Context:** The shift to online interactions during the COVID‑19 pandemic means that first impressions now often rely on virtual cues.
- **Visual Context:** Video backgrounds serve as a key non-verbal cue that can shape trait inferences. They may signal professionalism, personal environment, or even threat.
- **Theoretical Framework:** The study draws on models such as the Stereotype Content Model, highlighting warmth (trustworthiness) and competence as central dimensions in first impression formation.

## Method
### Participants and Stimuli
- **Participants:** A diverse sample participated in an online experiment where they rated first impressions.
- **Stimuli:** 
  - Facial images (male and female) displaying both happy and neutral expressions.
  - These images were overlaid onto six different virtual backgrounds (e.g., Home, Blurred Home, Bookcase, Plants, Blank, Novelty) to simulate a videoconference setting.
- **Design:** A within-participants design where each participant viewed all 72 face-background combinations. Ratings for trustworthiness and competence were collected on 7-point Likert scales.

### Procedure
- **Simulation of Videoconference:** Faces were presented with a Zoom-like border to enhance ecological validity.
- **Measurement:** After each stimulus, participants rated “How trustworthy is this individual?” and “How competent is this individual?”

## Results
- **Background Effects:**
  - **Trustworthiness:** Faces with backgrounds of Plants and Bookcase were rated as significantly more trustworthy than those with Home or Novelty backgrounds.
  - **Competence:** Similarly, backgrounds like Bookcase, Plants, and Blank resulted in higher competence ratings compared to Home, Blurred Home, and Novelty.
- **Facial Expression:**
  - Happy faces were rated much higher on both trustworthiness and competence than neutral faces.
  - There was an additive effect; across all backgrounds, happy expressions boosted ratings.
- **Gender Effects:**
  - Female faces received higher trustworthiness and competence ratings than male faces overall.
  - Interaction effects indicated that for certain backgrounds (e.g., Home), the negative impact on ratings was more pronounced for male faces.
- **Interaction Effects:**
  - A three-way interaction between background, facial expression, and gender was observed, suggesting that the influence of background cues can be moderated by both the facial expression presented and the gender of the face.

## Discussion
- **Implications for Videoconferencing:**
  - **Professionalism vs. Authenticity:** While traditional advice has advocated for neutral or professional backgrounds (e.g., blank walls) to project competence, the study finds that backgrounds like Plants and Bookcase can enhance perceptions of both competence and trustworthiness.
  - **Negative Cues:** Personal spaces (Home) or novelty backgrounds may evoke perceptions of informality or even secrecy, thereby reducing ratings.
- **Facial Expression Effects:** Smiling (or happy expressions) strongly improves first impressions, potentially offsetting some negative background effects.
- **Gender Considerations:** Female faces tend to be viewed more positively across dimensions; for male faces, background selection appears particularly critical.
- **Practical Recommendations:** 
  - In professional videoconferencing (e.g., interviews, business meetings), using backgrounds that feature elements like books or plants may optimize first impressions.
  - Users should consider using a “blurred” version of their home environment rather than showing the full personal space.

## Conclusion
- **Summary:** Video backgrounds are a significant non-verbal cue in videoconferencing that influence first impressions of trustworthiness and competence.
- **Practical Impact:** The findings underscore the importance of thoughtful background selection in virtual settings, with recommendations for individuals and organizations to adopt more professional backgrounds to make the best possible first impression.
- **Future Directions:** Additional research should further disentangle the complex interplay between background type, facial expressions, and gender—especially across diverse cultural contexts and with more varied background options.

## Key Information on Zoom Backgrounds and Their Effects on First Impressions

### Overview
- **Objective:**  
  This study investigates how the choice of video conferencing background influences viewers’ first impressions—specifically, judgments of trustworthiness and competence.
- **Context:**  
  The research is situated in the era of videoconferencing, where the COVID-19 pandemic forced many interactions online. It explores how non-verbal contextual cues, such as virtual backgrounds, affect trait inferences.
- **Method:**  
  The study employed experimental methods using controlled stimuli: faces (with varied facial expressions) superimposed onto different virtual backgrounds that simulate a Zoom interface. These backgrounds include settings like a home, a blurred home, a bookcase, house plants, a blank wall, and a novelty background.

### Impact of Zoom Backgrounds on Perception and Judgment

#### Trustworthiness and Competence Evaluations
- **Background Effects:**  
  The study found that the visual context provided by the background significantly affects perceptions of both trustworthiness and competence:
  - **Positive Backgrounds:**  
    Backgrounds featuring elements like plants and a bookcase yielded higher ratings on trust and competence.
  - **Negative Backgrounds:**  
    In contrast, backgrounds depicting a home environment or novelty settings consistently received lower ratings.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "Faces with a background of Home and Novelty were perceived as being significantly less trustworthy than those with backgrounds of Plants, Bookcase, Blurred Home, and Blank."

#### Interaction with Facial Expression
- **Role of Expression:**  
  Facial expressions further modulate these impressions:
  - Happy faces were judged as significantly more trustworthy and competent compared to neutral faces across all background types.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "Happy faces were perceived as significantly more trustworthy than neutral faces across all backgrounds, demonstrating an additive effect where positive facial expression can mitigate some of the negative cues from certain backgrounds."

#### Gender Influences
- **Gender Differences:**  
  The study observed a significant main effect of gender:
  - Female faces were generally rated as more trustworthy and more competent than male faces.
  - Interaction effects indicated that, for example, in certain backgrounds (such as Home), male faces received particularly lower trustworthiness ratings.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "Female faces were judged to be significantly more trustworthy than male faces across all backgrounds; notably, for male faces in the Home background, trustworthiness ratings dropped markedly."

### Implications and Recommendations

- **For Professionals and Job Candidates:**  
  - The findings suggest that selecting a virtual background that conveys professionalism (such as a bookcase or plant-filled setting) can positively influence first impressions in professional contexts, including interviews.
- **For Organizations:**  
  - Employers and meeting organizers might consider advising participants on optimal background choices to foster better perceptions of competence and trust.
- **Balancing Authenticity and Professionalism:**  
  - Although neutral or blank backgrounds can signal competence, overly personal or novel backgrounds might undermine perceptions of trustworthiness.
  - **Quote:**  
    > "The professional background should strike a balance—providing a cue of competence without sacrificing authenticity and trustworthiness."

*Summary:*  
This paper demonstrates that the choice of Zoom background plays a critical role in shaping first impressions in virtual meetings. Positive, professional-looking backgrounds (e.g., bookcase, plants) enhance perceptions of trust and competence, while personal or informal settings (e.g., home, novelty) may detract from these traits. Furthermore, facial expressions and gender interact with background choices to further influence these impressions. The study’s findings underscore the importance of careful background selection in videoconferencing contexts, with implications for both individual users and organizations.
