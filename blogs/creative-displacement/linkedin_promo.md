I get my best creative work done on a kitchen counter.

Not the home office with dual 4K monitors and the ergonomic chair. The kitchen counter. Laptop on a cutting board, portable monitor against the backsplash.

Turns out there's a decade of cognitive psychology research explaining why — and it challenges how we think about the WFH vs. office debate entirely.

→ Mednick's Associative Theory: novel environments flatten your mental shortcuts, forcing creative leaps
→ The "café effect": 70dB of ambient noise hits the exact sweet spot for abstract thinking
→ Constraint breeds creativity: fewer tools means your brain stops defaulting and starts inventing
→ Beethoven moved 60+ times. Rowling wrote Harry Potter in cafés while broke. The pattern isn't subtle.

The real question isn't where you should work. It's how often you should change where you work.
